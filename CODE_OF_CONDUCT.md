# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want this project to remain a constructive, respectful, and intellectually productive space for discussion and collaboration.

Contributors, users, and participants are expected to engage with one another in good faith, especially when discussing:
- methodology
- terminology
- localisation
- conceptual boundaries
- authorship
- professional practice

This project welcomes critique, disagreement, and revision.  
It does not welcome contempt, harassment, hostility, or dismissive behaviour.

---

## Expected behaviour

Please:
- be respectful in tone
- explain your reasoning when proposing changes
- distinguish criticism of ideas from criticism of people
- assume good faith unless there is strong reason not to
- remain open to clarification and correction
- respect linguistic and cultural differences in localisation work

---

## Unacceptable behaviour

The following are not acceptable:
- personal attacks
- insults or derogatory comments
- harassment
- discriminatory language
- bad-faith disruption
- repeated dismissive or hostile engagement
- deliberately misrepresenting another contributor’s position

---

## Methodological disagreement

This project includes conceptual and methodological material.  
Disagreement is normal and expected.

However:
- argue from reasoning, not status
- explain conceptual implications of proposed changes
- avoid turning wording debates into personal conflicts
- keep discussion focused on the framework and its use

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:
- issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- project-related communication in public repository spaces

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may:
- request clarification
- ask participants to adjust tone or behaviour
- close discussions that become unproductive
- remove clearly abusive contributions
- restrict participation in repeated cases of harmful conduct

---

## Final principle

The goal of this repository is not only to host code or text, but to support a serious, open, and collaborative discussion about human–AI work.

Please help keep it that way.
